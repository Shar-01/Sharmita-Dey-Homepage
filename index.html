About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Goettingen, specializing in AI, robotics, and human-machine interaction. My research spans applying continual learning, multitask learning, learning from simulated interactions, and multimodal learning in the context of adaptive learning for intelligent rehabilitation agents. I also worked on projects related to vision-based learning of the interactions and dynamics of moving objects from videos. I have collaborated with NASA JPL, focusing on autonomous exploration in perceptually-degraded environments.

Research Projects

Autonomous Terrain Navigation

Developed learning-based algorithms for terrain classification and autonomous navigation in dynamic environments, improving robot decision-making in perceptually degraded scenarios.

Learning-based Strategies for Adaptive Control of Rehabilitation Agents

Create adaptive models for rehabilitation agents that perceive user states through multimodal inputs and are adaptable to various tasks, temporal changes in gait patterns, terrains, and past prediction errors. Methods entail continual imitation learning, multitask learning, domain adaptation, self-supervised learning, and multimodal learning.

Generalized Decoding of Control Signals from Surface Electromyography Signals

This effort was aimed at creating a robust intent recognition model that remained effective regardless of variations in electrode placements, thus ensuring its seamless use daily. .

Learning Object Interactions and Dynamics from Videos

Understanding object interactions and dynamics from multi-object videos by disentangling their paths.
